Local scope: Urban mapping by GISAT

LC/LU mapping for 5 cities in Southeastern Asia

This dataset represents land use/land cover mapping on local level, with relatively high spatial detail. Both status and change mapping have been performed.

The dataset has been prepared by GISAT company, based on EO data, mostly satellite imagery. Details on this mapping, including mapping nomenclature, minimal mapping unit and mapping accuracy can be found in chapter 2.1 of PUMA Interim report. The dataset is intended for detailed analysis of urban land cover and changes, especially their internal structure (e.g. urban sprawl level).


In frame of this mapping, five metropolitan areas in Eastern Asia and Pacific region, defined by World Bank requirements, have been mapped. These territories are listed in the section for territory selection.


Beside this dataset, ancillary road and population data have been used, to demonstrate potential of land cover / land use and change analysis at this detailed level in connection with data from other sources. Population data for Surabaya administrative units have been provided by World Bank. For other four cities, populations for years 2000 and 2010 have were calculated from AsiaPop dataset.

For demonstration of analysis based on line datasets in PUMA platform, ancillary layer of road network have been incorporated into analyses. The source of this road data is mainly Open street map, which provided both vector geometry and information about road width category. Original width categories have been reclassified also into more general road types. For Surabaya Core City area, road data have been provided directly by World Bank.
