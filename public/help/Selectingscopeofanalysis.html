Setting the scope of analysis

The scope of geographical analysis represents the level of spatial and thematic detail of explored data.


You can choose between three available scopes in the menu. Following scopes are available in current version of the PUMA application:

  • Scope: Local provides the most detailed view into geographically based information of all available scopes. Results of urban mapping performed by GISAT are used as a data source for this scope.
  • Scope: National represents the middle-scale analysis at the level of particular countries. This scope is filled in by the Urban expansion dataset provided by Annemarie Schneider  team.
  • Scope: Regional is the scope at the lowest level of detail, which gives the user an overview of whole region (in this case East Asia and Pacific). AsiaPop population grids are the source data for this scope.
  • Scope: Global is an example of analysis on global geographical level, with coverage spread over significant part of African continent and a comparison with situation in an European city (represented by Paris area so far).
  • Scope: Global urban footprint shows an example of analysis based on Global urban footprint dataset. So far, this analysis is focused on Central American countries - Panama and Guatemala.


So far, each scope of analysis is represented only by one dataset. However, in general, more different datasets could be suitable for analysis in the same scope.
